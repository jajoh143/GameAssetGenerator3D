Target Style: Low-Poly Stylized Game Character

Reference guide for the GameAssetGenerator3D humanoid pipeline

TARGET — Clean colors, big expressive
eyes, clear silhouette, distinct elements
AVOID — Muted colors, tiny eyes,
no highlights, elements blend together

1. Proportions

The gold standard (Synty POLYGON, KayKit, Quaternius) uses slightly chibi proportions:

  • Head is ~25-30% of total height (our template mesh does this well)
  • Shorter legs relative to torso
  • Slightly oversized hands
  • Clean T-pose or A-pose for rigging
  • Simple blocky shapes — no realistic muscle definition

2. Face & Eyes

The face is the most important feature for character appeal:

  • Large, expressive eyes (oval, not circular) with white catchlight highlights
  • Eyes should be ~20-25% of face width
  • Some styles are completely faceless (Synty POLYGON) — just smooth head geometry
  • If eyes exist, they need to be BIG and read clearly at game camera distance
  • Simple eyebrows for expression
  • Tiny or no nose; simple curve or no mouth

3. Color Palette

Colors should be vibrant, saturated, and clearly distinct per body part:

  • Each element (hair, skin, shirt, pants, shoes) must be a DIFFERENT hue
  • Warm skin tones with subtle sheen (not flat matte)
  • Clothing colors should pop — reds, blues, greens at 70-85% saturation
  • Dark hair for light skin (contrast frames the face)
  • Single flat color per material — no textures needed
  • 2-4 materials total per character

4. Materials & Shading

The "toy-like" or "vinyl figure" look comes from smooth-but-faceted shading:

  • Roughness: 0.35-0.55 (smooth enough for subtle specularity)
  • Light specular highlights (Specular IOR 0.3-0.5)
  • Edge split modifier at 30-40° for crisp polygon facets
  • No bump maps, normal maps, or complex textures
  • Flat colors from a shared texture atlas OR vertex colors
  • Slight subsurface scattering on skin (optional)

5. Clothing Fit

Clothing must sit flush against the body — this is our biggest current issue:

  • Clothing should be slightly larger than body mesh, NOT floating
  • Best approach: clothing as vertex-painted regions on the SAME mesh
  • Alternative: clothing shells that tightly conform to body shape
  • Ring-based procedural clothing needs careful radius calibration to template
  • Gaps between clothing and body destroy the silhouette

6. Lighting & Presentation

Good presentation makes or breaks the asset showcase:

  • 3-point lighting: warm key + cool fill + rim light
  • Neutral or gradient background (not flat white/black)
  • Ground plane with soft shadow for grounding
  • 65-85mm lens equivalent for flattering proportions
  • Front + 3/4 view for turnaround reference
